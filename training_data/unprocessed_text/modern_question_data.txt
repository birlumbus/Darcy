
General Knowledge

    What is the speed of light?
        ~299,792,458 meters per second (in a vacuum).
    Can you explain quantum entanglement?
        When two particles become linked, their states remain correlated regardless of distance, meaning measuring one instantly affects the other.
    What are the major causes of climate change?
        Greenhouse gas emissions (CO₂, methane), deforestation, industrialization, and fossil fuel burning.
    How do black holes form?
        When massive stars collapse under their own gravity after exhausting nuclear fuel, forming a singularity with an event horizon.

Science & Technology

    What is the difference between RAM and ROM?
        RAM (Random Access Memory) is volatile and temporary; ROM (Read-Only Memory) is non-volatile and stores permanent data.
    What is the Turing Test?
        A test to determine if a machine can exhibit human-like intelligence by convincing a human it is also human.
    Why do planes fly?
        Lift is generated by air moving faster over the curved upper surface of wings, creating lower pressure above and higher pressure below.
    How does a nuclear reactor generate power?
        The nucleus of an atom can be split into two smaller fragments with a neutron. This process generates immense heat. That heat is used to produce steam that drives turbines connected to generators.

History & Politics

    What caused the fall of the Roman Empire?
        Political corruption, economic troubles, military defeats, overexpansion, and barbarian invasions.
    Who were the key players in World War II?
        Allies: USA, USSR, UK, China, France. Axis: Germany, Japan, Italy.
    What was the Cold War about?
        A geopolitical struggle (1947–1991) between the US (capitalism) and the USSR (communism), involving proxy wars, espionage, and nuclear arms race.
    How did the Industrial Revolution change society?
        It shifted economies from agriculture to industry, increased urbanization, improved technology, and altered social structures.
    What are the fundamental principles of democracy?
        Popular sovereignty, rule of law, individual rights, majority rule with minority rights, and free/fair elections.

Philosophy & Ethics

    What is the meaning of life?
        Philosophers, religions, and scientists offer different views—some say it's subjective, others propose purpose through knowledge, relationships, or contribution.
    Do humans have free will?
        Debated in philosophy and science; determinists argue actions are caused by prior events, while compatibilists and libertarians believe in some level of choice.
    Is it ever morally justifiable to lie?
        Some argue yes (e.g., to protect someone), while others uphold truth as absolute. Ethical perspectives vary, such as utilitarianism vs. deontology.
    What is the trolley problem?
        A moral dilemma where one must choose between pulling a lever to divert a trolley, killing one person to save five, or doing nothing and letting five die.
    What are the main branches of philosophy?
        Metaphysics (existence), Epistemology (knowledge), Ethics (morality), Aesthetics (art/beauty), Logic (reasoning), and Political Philosophy (governance).

Literature & Arts

    What is the significance of Shakespeare’s works?
        His plays and sonnets deeply influenced literature, language, and drama, exploring timeless themes like power, love, fate, and human nature.
    How did the Renaissance impact art?
        It introduced realism, perspective, humanism, and individualism, leading to masterpieces by artists like Leonardo da Vinci and Michelangelo.
    What are common themes in dystopian fiction?
        Oppressive governments, loss of individuality, surveillance, environmental decay, technological control, and resistance against authority.
    Who are some influential surrealist painters?
        Salvador Dalí, René Magritte, Max Ernst, and Joan Miró, known for dreamlike, irrational, and symbolic imagery.

Mathematics & Logic

    What is the Fibonacci sequence?
        A sequence where each number is the sum of the two preceding ones: 0, 1, 1, 2, 3, 5, 8, 13... It appears in nature, art, and mathematics.
    How does calculus differ from algebra?
        Algebra deals with solving equations and manipulating symbols, while calculus focuses on rates of change (derivatives) and accumulation (integrals).
    What is Gödel’s incompleteness theorem?
        It states that in any consistent formal mathematical system, there are true statements that cannot be proven within the system itself.
    Why is pi an irrational number?
        Because it cannot be expressed as a fraction of two integers; its decimal representation goes on infinitely without repeating.

Psychology & Sociology

    What is Maslow’s hierarchy of needs?
        A psychological theory ranking human needs from basic (physiological, safety) to higher (love, esteem, self-actualization).
    What is cognitive dissonance?
        A psychological discomfort from holding conflicting beliefs or behaviors, often leading to attitude changes.
    How does conditioning work in psychology?
        Through classical conditioning (associating stimuli, like Pavlov’s dogs) and operant conditioning (reinforcement or punishment shaping behavior).
    What are the effects of social media on mental health?
        Can cause anxiety, depression, and low self-esteem, but also fosters connection and self-expression. Impact depends on usage patterns.

Pop Culture & Entertainment

    How has science fiction influenced real-world technology?
        Inspired innovations like cell phones (Star Trek), AI (Asimov), touchscreens, robots, and space exploration.
    What are the key elements of a compelling story?
        Strong characters, engaging conflict, clear stakes, immersive world-building, and emotional depth.
    How do film genres evolve over time?
        They adapt to cultural changes, audience preferences, and technological advancements (e.g., silent films to CGI-heavy blockbusters).
    What makes a great protagonist?
        Complexity, relatability, growth, clear motivation, and the ability to drive the story forward.
    How does music impact mood and emotions?
        It influences emotions through tempo, lyrics, and melody, triggering memories, reducing stress, or boosting energy.

Everyday Life & Practical Knowledge

    How do credit scores work?
        A numerical rating based on credit history, debt levels, payment habits, and loan diversity, affecting loan approvals and interest rates.
    What are effective ways to learn a new language?
        Immersion, spaced repetition, conversation practice, consuming media in the language, and daily exposure.
    How can you develop critical thinking skills?
        Question assumptions, analyze evidence, consider multiple perspectives, and practice logical reasoning.

Miscellaneous & Hypotheticals

    What would happen if gravity suddenly disappeared?
        Everything not anchored would float away, Earth's atmosphere would dissipate, and planetary orbits would destabilize.
    How would humanity react to first contact with aliens?
        Likely a mix of awe, fear, scientific curiosity, and geopolitical tension, depending on the nature of the encounter.
    What is the most important invention in human history?
        The wheel, electricity, printing press, internet, and vaccines are strong contenders, depending on criteria.
    If you could change one law of physics, what would happen?
        Altering gravity, time, or thermodynamics could lead to chaos, like floating planets, infinite energy, or paradoxes.
    How would society change if humans never needed sleep?
        More productivity, longer work hours, extended leisure, potential mental health effects, and a restructured economy.
